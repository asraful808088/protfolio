About Me
Discover My Journey
Full-Stack Developer & AI Explorer

I began my coding journey in 2021. Over the span of 14-16 months, I diligently acquired proficiency in 5-6 programming languages including C, C++, Java, Python, JavaScript, and Dart. My approach has always been to deeply understand programming concepts and the underlying principles, leveraging a variety of learning resources.

This methodical approach led me to explore numerous front-end and back-end frameworks. Initially, I learned raw Node.js with Express, which helped me grasp about 30% of back-end development concepts. Progressing to Django, I gained insights into file distribution, management systems, and system design. Finally, mastering Laravel significantly enhanced my understanding of server-side operations and management. Additionally, I delved into Go and Rust with Actix servers, and front-end technologies like React, Next.js, Vue.js, and Angular, along with state management libraries such as Redux, NgRx, Vuex, and MobX.

Beyond web development, I have extensive experience with Flutter and its state management libraries like Provider, BLoC, and RxDart. While I also explored Java Android development, I found it less appealing compared to other frameworks, focusing mainly on MVC (Model View Controller) and MVVM (Model View ViewModel) patterns. My passion extends to machine learning, where I have basic experience and have attempted to create chatbot builder tools akin to Google Dialogflow.

Note

The skill ratings below are based on my regular working experience, not how much of the full documentation I’ve studied. For example, a 3/5 in Laravel doesn’t mean I’ve only learned 60% — it means I’ve learned the most commonly used parts and use them regularly. The rest, I often pick up from online resources while working — which helps me solve problems in real time.

I always focus on learning the core concepts and fundamentals first. Since I’ve explored many tech stacks, it can be hard to balance everything at once. But whenever I start a project, I quickly remember and apply what I need. Most of my learning comes from real-world projects and practical problem-solving.
